About Me

I am a DPhil student in Machine Learning at the Oxford-Man Institute, with a background in mathematics, statistics and data science. Working under the supervision of Dr. Xiaowen Dong and Prof. Stephen Roberts, my focus is in machine learning models on graphs. Currently, I am working with kernel functions on graph spaces for Gaussian processes applied to various graph prediction problems, where models are designed to make use of various graph convolutions and exhibit transductive qualities for superior performances. I was awarded a PhD studentship from the EPSRC Doctoral Training Partnership (DTP) scheme.

I obtained First Class honours MSci in Mathematics at Imperial College London. In addition to machine learning, I have interests in a range of topics including Monte Carlo and Quasi Monte Carlo, option pricing and stochastic simulation.
